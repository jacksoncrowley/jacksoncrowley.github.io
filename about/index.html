About Me

I’m an Australian PhD Student in computational biophysics at the Molecular Microbology and Structural Biochemistry (MMSB) unit of the Centre national de la recherche scientifique (CNRS), **based in Lyon, France.

Specifically, I work on atomistic and coarse-grained molecular dynamics simulations of lipid droplets (and more). The biogenesis of these tiny organelles, fundamental to energy metabolism within the cell, is extremely difficult to study expexperimentally, due to their highly fluid nature and the fact that they often only consists of a few hundred molecules when they are first created.

Speaking more broadly, I’m interested in the fields of multiscale modelling and non-equilibrium simulations, or rather, anything that allows us to build more computational models to study biology in ways that cannot otherwise be done. As a classically trained biologist, I want to see how computational and experimental techniques can be combined to understand more about complex biological phenomena, specifically those that have implications in health and disease.

I hold an MPhil with Honours in Biomedical Engineering from UTS, of which I spent the second year doing full time research into the interactions of steroid molecules with biological membranes using both MD simulations and experimental techniques. Prior to that, I recieved a Bachelor of Medical Sciences degree from Macquarie University, with a focus on biochemistry and molecular biology.
